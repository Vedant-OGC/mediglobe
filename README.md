## 1. DOCUMENTS REQUIRED FOR APPROVAL AND LICENSING

### 1.1 Company Registration Documents
- **Certificate of Incorporation** from the Registrar of Companies (ROC)
- **Memorandum of Association (MOA)** and **Articles of Association (AOA)**
- **PAN Card** of the company
- **TAN (Tax Deduction Account Number)**
- **GST Registration Certificate**
- **Udyam Registration** (MSME Registration, if applicable)
- **Import Export Code (IEC)** if facilitating international payments or services

### 1.2 Medical Tourism Specific Licenses

#### A. Tourism License
- **Travel Agency License** from the Ministry of Tourism
  - Application form with company details
  - Proof of office premises (rent agreement/ownership documents)
  - Bank solvency certificate (minimum ₹2-5 lakhs depending on category)
  - Surety bond or financial security
  - Professional qualifications of key personnel
  - Insurance coverage details

#### B. Healthcare Facilitation Permits
- **Medical Value Travel (MVT) Facilitator Registration** (if applicable in your state)
- **NOC from State Health Department** for coordinating medical services
- **Data Protection Registration** under Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023

#### C. Hospital Partnerships & Accreditations
- **MOU agreements** with NABH/JCI accredited hospitals
- **Service Level Agreements (SLAs)** with healthcare providers
- **Verification certificates** from partner hospitals
- **Insurance empanelment documents** (if providing insurance facilitation)

### 1.3 Financial and Legal Documents
- **Business Bank Account Details** and statements
- **Professional Indemnity Insurance** policy (minimum coverage: ₹10-25 lakhs)
- **General Liability Insurance** for business operations
- **Cyber Insurance** (for data protection)
- **Audited Financial Statements** (if existing business)
- **Tax Compliance Certificates** (ITR for last 2-3 years)

### 1.4 Data Protection and Privacy
- **Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)** document
- **Privacy Policy** compliant with DPDP Act 2023
- **Data Processing Agreement** templates
- **Consent Management Framework** documentation
- **Cybersecurity Policy** and incident response plan

### 1.5 Quality and Compliance Certifications (Recommended)
- **ISO 9001:2015** (Quality Management System)
- **ISO 27001** (Information Security Management)
- **Medical Tourism Association (MTA)** certification (international)
- **MTQUA** (Medical Tourism Quality Alliance) accreditation

## 4. SOFTWARE PROCUREMENT STRATEGY

### 4.1 Build vs. Buy Decision Framework

#### When to BUY (Subscription/Off-the-shelf)
- **Standard functions**: CRM, accounting, communication tools
- **Advantages**: Immediate deployment, regular updates, lower initial cost
- **Recommended for**: CRM, email marketing, accounting, video conferencing

#### When to BUILD (Custom Development)
- **Unique processes**: Patient journey management, hospital integration
- **Advantages**: Tailored to business needs, competitive advantage, data control
- **Recommended for**: Patient portal, hospital interface, booking system

### 4.2 Software Procurement Process

#### For Subscription Software (SaaS)

**Step 1: Requirements Analysis (Week 1-2)**
- Define must-have features
- Identify integration needs
- Determine number of users
- Set budget constraints

**Step 2: Vendor Research (Week 3-4)**
- Shortlist 3-5 vendors
- Request demos and trials
- Compare features and pricing
- Check customer reviews and references

**Step 3: Trial and Evaluation (Week 5-7)**
- Free trial with actual workflows
- Test integrations
- Involve end-users in evaluation
- Assess customer support quality

**Step 4: Negotiation and Purchase (Week 8)**
- Negotiate pricing (especially for annual contracts)
- Review SLA and terms of service
- Check data ownership and exit clauses
- Confirm DPDP Act compliance

**Step 5: Implementation (Week 9-12)**
- Data migration (if applicable)
- User training
- Integration with existing systems
- Pilot testing before full rollout

#### For Custom Software Development

**Step 1: Requirement Specification (Month 1)**
- Detailed functional requirements document
- User stories and use cases
- Technical specifications
- Data security requirements

**Step 2: Vendor Selection (Month 2)**
- RFP (Request for Proposal) to development agencies
- Evaluate portfolios and expertise in healthcare IT
- Interview 3-5 shortlisted vendors
- Check references from previous healthcare clients
- **Estimated cost**: ₹8,00,000 - ₹25,00,000 depending on complexity

**Step 3: Development Contract (Month 2)**
- Fixed-price or time-and-material contract
- Milestone-based payments
- IP rights and ownership clauses
- Maintenance and support terms
- **Typical payment**: 30% advance, 40% on milestones, 30% on delivery

**Step 4: Development and Testing (Month 3-6)**
- Agile sprints with bi-weekly reviews
- User acceptance testing (UAT)
- Security audits and penetration testing
- Performance optimization

**Step 5: Deployment and Maintenance (Month 7+)**
- Phased rollout
- Staff training
- Bug fixes and updates
- **Annual maintenance**: 15-20% of development cost

### 4.3 Recommended Technology Stack

#### For Custom Development
- **Frontend**: React.js/Angular for web portal
- **Backend**: Node.js/Python (Django/Flask)
- **Database**: PostgreSQL/MySQL for relational data
- **Cloud Hosting**: AWS/Azure/Google Cloud
- **Security**: SSL certificates, encryption, HIPAA-compliant hosting
